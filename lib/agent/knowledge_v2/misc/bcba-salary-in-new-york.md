We’ll also explore how much a BCBA makes a year, BCBA salary by state comparisons, and whether it’s a good career path. What Is a Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA)? A BCBA is a certified professional specializing in applied behavior analysis (ABA), designing and implementing interventions to improve behaviors, often for individuals with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). To become a BCBA, you need a master’s degree, supervised fieldwork, and certification from the Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB). In New York, BCBAs work in settings like clinics, schools, and in-home therapy, helping clients build skills in communication, social interaction, and daily living.The role demands expertise in data-driven strategies, making it rewarding for those passionate about behavioral health. As demand grows, so does the certified behavior analyst salary, particularly in high-need areas like New York City (NYC).
