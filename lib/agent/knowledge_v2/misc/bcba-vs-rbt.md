If you’re exploring a career in Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA), you’ve probably come across two key roles: BCBA and RBT. Both positions are essential for helping individuals with autism and other developmental needs, but their responsibilities, training requirements, and career paths differ significantly. Understanding these differences will help you choose the role that aligns with your professional goals and lifestyle. What is a BCBA (Board Certified Behavior Analyst)? A Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) is a graduate-level professional who designs, supervises, and monitors ABA therapy programs. They are responsible for conducting assessments, creating behavior intervention plans (BIPs), and training RBTs and caregivers to implement strategies effectively. What is an RBT (Registered Behavior Technician)? A Registered Behavior Technician (RBT) is a paraprofessional who delivers ABA therapy directly to clients under the supervision of a BCBA. RBTs implement behavior plans, collect data, and reinforce skills, but they do not create intervention plans or make independent clinical decisions.RBT Certification RequirementsTo become an RBT, you must:Complete a 40-hour RBT training program.Pass a competency assessment with a qualified BCBA.Maintain certification with ongoing supervision and renewals every year.RBTs are often the frontline providers in ABA, working closely with children and families to reinforce learning in natural environments.Love direct, hands-on client work?
