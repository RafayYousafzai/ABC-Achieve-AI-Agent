What Is a BCBA and What Do They Do? A Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) is a highly trained professional who designs and supervises behavior intervention plans. These specialists conduct assessments, analyze data, and lead a team of Behavior Technicians (BTs) or Registered Behavior Technicians (RBTs) to ensure the effective delivery of ABA therapy. Their work positively impacts children and adults facing developmental and behavioral challenges, particularly those with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Speak with an Advisor Step 1: Understand the Role and Requirements Before diving into the process, it’s important to understand what becoming a BCBA entails. The position requires a strong grasp of behavioral theory, a compassionate mindset, and leadership abilities to guide treatment teams. Step 2: Educational Pathway To become a BCBA, you must first complete a relevant degree. According to the BCBA certification requirements: Earn a bachelor’s degree in psychology, education, or a related field Pursue a master’s degree in behavior analysis, education, or psychology from an accredited university Make sure the graduate program includes verified course sequences approved by the Behavior Analyst Certification Board (BACB) Some universities offer standalone BCBA certification programs for those who already have a qualifying master’s degree. Step 3: Gain Experience as a Behavior Technician (BT) A great starting point in the ABA field is working as a Behavior Technician. This role gives you hands-on experience and introduces you to the field of ABA. Step 4: Accumulate Supervised Fieldwork Hours The next key step to BCBA certification is completing supervised fieldwork. The BACB outlines specific requirements for this stage: 2,000 hours of supervised fieldwork, or 1,500 hours of concentrated supervised fieldwork Supervision must be conducted by a qualified BCBA who monitors your performance and provides feedback. It is important to work in diverse environments to gain broad experience with different populations and case types. Step 5: Apply for BCBA Certification Once you complete your degree and supervised hours, it’s time to apply for the BCBA certification examination. This involves: Submitting your academic transcripts Providing documentation of completed supervised fieldwork Paying the BACB application and exam fees The certification exam covers all key areas of behavior analysis, including measurement, assessment, intervention, and ethical practices. Ready to Launch Your BCBA Career Today? Apply for BT / BCBA Pathway Step 6: Pass the BCBA Exam The BCBA exam is a multiple-choice test that evaluates your competency in applied behavior analysis. Success on this exam is necessary to earn your official title and begin practicing independently. Many candidates benefit from structured study plans, exam prep courses, and peer study groups to ensure success. ABS encourages continuous learning and offers support to team members preparing for this critical milestone. Step 7: Maintain Certification with Continuing Education Once you are certified, you must keep your skills and knowledge up-to-date through ongoing professional development. This includes earning Continuing Education Units (CEUs) and adhering to the BACB’s code of ethics. Staying current ensures your ability to provide high-quality services and stay in compliance with professional standards. How Long Does It Take to Become a BCBA? The journey from entry to certification typically takes between 6 to 8 years, depending on your education path and how quickly you accumulate supervised hours. For example: Bachelor’s degree: 4 years Master’s degree: 2 years Supervised fieldwork: 1-2 years (can be concurrent with your master’s program) Career Outlook for BCBAs BCBA-certified professionals are in high demand across schools, clinics, hospitals, and private practices.
