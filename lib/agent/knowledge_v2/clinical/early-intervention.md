Read More The Importance of Early Diagnosis and Intervention Read More Early Intervention ABA Therapy FAQs We’ve got answers — dive into our FAQs below! What is Early Intervention ABA therapy? Early Intervention ABA therapy is a specialized approach designed to support young children, typically from birth to age three, who have developmental delays or autism spectrum disorder. The goal is to build communication, social, and learning skills during the most critical years of brain development. What are the 5 pillars of Early Intervention ABA therapy? The 5 pillars often include early assessment, individualized treatment planning, skill-building, parent involvement, and consistent progress monitoring. Does Achievement Behavior Services provide in-home Early Intervention ABA therapy? Yes. ABS offers personalized in-home Early Intervention ABA therapy, making it easier for children to learn in a familiar and comfortable environment while empowering parents to be actively involved in the process. How does ABS customize Early Intervention programs? At ABS, we create individualized treatment plans based on each child’s strengths, needs, and family goals. Our Board Certified Behavior Analysts (BCBAs) work closely with families to ensure therapy is engaging, effective, and tailored to the child’s unique developmental journey. Can Early Intervention ABA therapy help with speech and communication delays? Yes. Early Intervention ABA focuses on improving communication skills, which may include speech, sign language, or alternative communication methods, depending on the child’s needs. What are the 3 main goals of Early Intervention? They are improving communication, building independence in daily living, and enhancing social interaction skills.
