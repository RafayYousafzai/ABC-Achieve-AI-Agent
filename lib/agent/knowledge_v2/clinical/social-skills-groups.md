What are ABA-based Social Skills Groups? Social Skills Groups use ABA principles to teach children how to interact with peers, communicate effectively, and navigate social situations through structured activities and guided practice. Who can benefit from Social Skills Groups? These groups are often recommended for children and adolescents with autism or social communication challenges who need support building peer relationships and social confidence. Are Social Skills Groups part of ABA therapy? Yes. Social Skills Groups are typically integrated into a child’s broader ABA treatment plan and are supervised by a Board Certified Behavior Analyst. Do Social Skills Groups help with school and friendships? Yes. Skills practiced in group sessions are designed to generalize to classrooms, playgrounds, and everyday social settings.
