The Origins of ABA Therapy Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) therapy has its roots in the early 20th century with the pioneering work of B.F. Skinner. Skinner’s research on operant conditioning laid the foundation for ABA. The term “Applied Behavior Analysis” was formally introduced in 1968, marking the establishment of the field. Since then, ABA has evolved into a highly effective approach for modifying behaviors and improving the quality of life for individuals with autism. Insurance Coverage and Accessibility A significant milestone in the ABA industry is the expansion of insurance coverage.
